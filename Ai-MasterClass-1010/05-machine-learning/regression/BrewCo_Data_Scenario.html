WWWWW-H Framework — Understanding the Data
👤
WHO
Who are the subjects of this data?
3,500 registered customers across Algeria — mostly urban, aged 18–45, who purchased at least once between Jan 2023 and Dec 2024. Includes both individuals and small café businesses.
Individual buyers Small café owners 18–45 age range 48 wilayas
📦
WHAT
What information is recorded?
Each row = one order line. The dataset stores the product purchased, quantity, unit price, total amount, customer ID, payment method, and order status (delivered / returned / pending).
Order details Products & prices Payment method Order status
📅
WHEN
What time period does it cover?
January 2023 → December 2024 — 2 full calendar years. Includes Ramadan periods, summer peak, and Eid holiday spikes. Each order has an exact timestamp (date + hour).
2 full years Ramadan seasons Hourly timestamps 87K orders
📍
WHERE
Where was the data collected?
Orders placed via the BrewCo website (65%), mobile app (22%), and 3 physical stores (13%) in Algiers, Oran, and Constantine. Delivery recorded per wilaya.
Website 65% Mobile App 22% In-store 13% 48 wilayas
🎯
WHY
Why does this data exist?
BrewCo collects this data to reduce customer churn, optimise inventory (avoid stockouts during Ramadan), personalise recommendations, and grow to 10,000 customers by 2025.
Reduce churn Stock optimisation Personalisation Growth target
⚙️
HOW
How was this data collected? — 4 automatic systems, zero manual entry
🌐
Website Tracking
Every click, cart, checkout logged. Timestamp + device + browser captured automatically.
🏪
POS System
Point-of-sale terminal at each store. Every scan generates a transaction record instantly.
📱
CRM Platform
Links customer history across all channels. Tracks lifetime value, frequency, last purchase.
🚚
Delivery App
Yalidine / Maystro API. Captures wilaya, delivery time, return reasons automatically.

6 Meaningful Insights We Can Extract
INSIGHT 01
Best-Selling Products by Wilaya
Which products are most popular in each city? Algiers may prefer capsules (fast, urban lifestyle) while Constantine may prefer whole-bean blends (traditional coffee culture).
📊 Chart: Grouped Bar Chart
df.groupby(['wilaya','product_name'])['quantity'].sum()
→ Algiers: Capsules 42% | Constantine: Blend 500g 61%
groupbybar chartpivot table
INSIGHT 02
Peak Order Hours & Days
When do customers actually order? In Algeria, expect a Ramadan spike after Iftar (9–11 PM). Knowing peak times optimises delivery staff and server load.
📈 Chart: Heatmap (Hour × Day of Week)
df['hour'] = df['order_date'].dt.hour
→ Peak: Thursday 9 PM (pre-weekend Iftar)
dt.hourheatmappivot
INSIGHT 03
Loyal vs One-Time Customers
What % of revenue comes from repeat customers? If 80% of revenue comes from 20% of customers, BrewCo should invest in loyalty programs rather than new acquisition.
🍩 Chart: Pie Chart + Bar Chart
df['is_repeat_customer'].value_counts()
→ 68% repeat | 32% one-time | Repeat = 84% of revenue
value_countspie chartsegmentation
INSIGHT 04
Payment Method Preferences
Does Algeria still rely on Cash on Delivery? Knowing this helps BrewCo decide whether to invest in Dahabia / CIB online payment infrastructure or keep COD as primary.
🍩 Chart: Donut Chart
df['payment_method'].value_counts(normalize=True)
→ COD: 71% | CIB Online: 19% | Dahabia: 10%
value_countsdonut chartnormalize
INSIGHT 05
Return Rate by Product
Which products get returned most? Negative quantity = return. High return rate on a product signals quality issues, misleading description, or packaging problems.
📊 Chart: Horizontal Bar Chart
returns = df[df['quantity'] < 0]
→ Brewing Kit: 14% return rate ← needs investigation
filteringbar chartquality control
INSIGHT 06
Seasonal Demand Patterns
How does revenue change across the year? Ramadan = massive spike. Summer = drop. Eid = gift sets peak. BrewCo needs 3× inventory during Ramadan or will lose sales.
📈 Chart: Line Chart (Monthly Revenue)
df.groupby(df['order_date'].dt.month)['total_amount'].sum()
→ March (Ramadan) = 3.2× average month revenue
